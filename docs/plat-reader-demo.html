Reading a survey plat with a local vision model

Lifting rotated metes-and-bounds labels off a scanned plat — and checking them — on one 8 GB GPU, no cloud.

A finished plat hides its survey data in plain sight. Every boundary course carries a quadrant bearing in degrees-minutes-seconds and a distance, drawn rotated to run along the line. A surveyor reads them at a glance. Optical character recognition does not — and that gap is the whole problem.

Act 2 — how it reads

Trace the traverse, deskew each course

The full sheet is cut into overlapping tiles, and each is read on its own. Here a reader walks the boundary course by course; each rotated label is recognised, deskewed to a clean value, and collected. When the last course lands, the traverse closes — the surveyor’s own check that the numbers are trustworthy, no CAD licence required.

    What you just watched

    That is the real pipeline: sectorise the sheet into overlapping tiles (overlap so a label on a seam is never split), read each tile with a vision model under one strict rule — transcribe the line dimensions at any rotation, and omit anything illegible rather than guess — collect the results into structured {bearing, distance} records, then check them against ground truth: the surveyor’s field notes, and the drawing’s own geometry (a read distance should match a recovered line length; a read bearing should match its azimuth).

    Recall is understated: several correct reads are interior subdivision courses (the 1/16 lines) that aren’t in the exterior field-note key at all. The honesty rule matters more than the score — a blank is safe, a fabricated bearing is dangerous. Earlier a weaker model was caught parroting an example value on every tile it couldn’t read; the example was removed so failures fail safe.

    A teaching demo for the plat2json local-VLM reader. Sample geometry is generic; the bearings, distances, and OCR failure modes are taken from a real BLM cadastral plat and the project’s own results. Runs on Qwen2.5-VL-7B via llama.cpp on an RTX 4060.
